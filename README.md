# Economic & Health Factors Influencing National Happiness  
## A Cross-Country Analysis (2022)

---

## Project Overview

This project explores how economic prosperity and health outcomes relate to national happiness across countries.

Using global development datasets, the analysis examines whether GDP per capita and life expectancy significantly influence Happiness Scores reported in the World Happiness Report (2022).

The project focuses on structured dataset merging, cleaning, and correlation visualization to extract meaningful global insights.

---

## Objective

- Analyze the relationship between GDP per capita and Happiness Score  
- Study the impact of Life Expectancy on national well-being  
- Identify patterns among the top happiest countries  

---

## Data Sources

- **World Happiness Report (2022)** – Happiness Score  
- **World Development Indicators (World Bank, 2022)** – GDP per capita, Life Expectancy  

The datasets were merged by country to enable cross-variable comparison.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- Google Sheets  
- Google Drive  
- Data Cleaning & Lookup Functions  
- Scatter Plot Visualization  

---

## Data Preparation

- Resolved country-name mismatches across datasets  
- Standardized country identifiers  
- Merged datasets using structured lookup techniques  
- Filtered relevant variables for analysis  

Data cleaning was critical to ensure accurate cross-country comparison.

---

## Analysis & Key Insights

### GDP per Capita vs Happiness Score

- A strong positive relationship was observed between GDP per capita and happiness levels.  
- Higher-income nations generally reported higher Happiness Scores.  

**Insight:** Economic prosperity plays a major role in overall national well-being.

---

### Life Expectancy vs Happiness Score

- Life expectancy shows a strong positive correlation with happiness.  
- Countries with better health outcomes tend to report higher satisfaction levels.  

**Insight:** Health and longevity significantly influence societal well-being.

---

### Top 10 Happiest Countries

- The top-ranked happiest countries are predominantly high-income nations.  
- These countries also demonstrate high life expectancy metrics.  

**Insight:** Economic stability and public health systems contribute substantially to happiness outcomes.

---

## Limitations

- The study uses cross-sectional data from a single year (2022).  
- Time-series trends were not analyzed due to limited standardized historical happiness data.  
- Social, governance, and cultural factors were not quantitatively evaluated.

---

## Key Takeaways

- GDP per capita and life expectancy are strongly associated with national happiness.  
- Economic growth alone does not fully explain happiness differences.  
- Multi-factor analysis is necessary to understand overall societal well-being.

This project demonstrates practical experience in dataset merging, data cleaning, and cross-country correlation analysis using real-world development indicators.
